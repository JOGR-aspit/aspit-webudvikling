The Library

Investigating Mr. Grayson

A Room Full of Secrets

You enter the library. Floor-to-ceiling bookshelves line the walls, filled with leather-bound volumes. A large fireplace crackles in the corner, casting flickering shadows across the room. The air smells of old paper and pipe tobacco.

Mr. Grayson stands by the window, his back straight as a ruler. He's a tall man with graying hair and a stiff posture. "May I help you, Detective?" he asks, his voice unusually formal.

Searching for Clues

While Mr. Grayson watches, you carefully examine the room. Near the fireplace, you notice something glinting in the light.

It's a small muddy footprint - too small to belong to any of the adult suspects. The footprint leads from a side door toward the fireplace.

"That door leads to the garden," Mr. Grayson explains. "It's usually locked, but..." He pauses. "I may have forgotten to lock it yesterday evening."

Clues Discovered

  1. Small muddy footprint - Near the fireplace, leading from garden door
  2. Unlocked garden door - Mr. Grayson admits he may have left it open
  3. Pipe tobacco smell - Fresh, suggesting someone was here recently

A Choice to Make

You could accuse Mr. Grayson right now - after all, he admitted leaving the door unlocked. But something doesn't feel right. The footprint is too small to be his.

What will you do?

What Do You Do?

  • Accuse Mr. Grayson of the theft - He left the door unlocked!
  • Investigate the garden - Follow the muddy footprints outside
