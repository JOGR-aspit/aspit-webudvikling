The Missing Heirloom

A Mystery Story

The Case Begins

You are Detective Morgan, a private investigator known for solving the most puzzling cases. Your phone rings early on a rainy Tuesday morning.

"Please, you must help me!" cries a voice on the other end. "My family heirloom has been stolen! It's a diamond brooch worth a fortune, but more importantly, it's been in my family for five generations."

The voice belongs to Mrs. Eleanor Blackwood, a wealthy widow who lives in Blackwood Manor on the hill outside of town. You agree to take the case.

Arriving at Blackwood Manor

An hour later, you stand before the grand entrance of Blackwood Manor. The old mansion towers above you, its windows watching like hollow eyes. Rain patters against your coat as you knock on the heavy wooden door.

Mrs. Blackwood greets you personally. She's an elderly woman with silver hair and worried eyes. "The brooch was in my jewelry box in the master bedroom," she explains. "I discovered it missing this morning. No one else knew where I kept it."

The Suspects

Mrs. Blackwood introduces you to the household staff. There are three people who had access to the house:

  • Mr. Grayson - The butler, who has served the family for 20 years
  • Rose - The young maid, who cleans the entire house daily
  • Tom - The gardener, who was trimming hedges near the windows yesterday

Mrs. Blackwood also mentions her beloved cat Mittens, a curious orange tabby who roams freely through the house.

Where Do You Start Your Investigation?

You need to gather clues. Where will you look first?

  • Investigate the library - Mr. Grayson spends most of his time here
  • Investigate the kitchen - Rose was cleaning near the master bedroom
