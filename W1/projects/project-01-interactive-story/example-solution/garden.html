The Garden

Following the Trail

Into the Rain

You step outside into the garden. The rain has stopped, leaving everything damp and glistening. A stone path winds through rose bushes and trimmed hedges. At the far end, you can see a small wooden garden shed.

Tom, the gardener, is pruning a bush nearby. He's a weathered man in his fifties, with rough hands and a friendly face. "Looking for clues, Detective?" he calls out. "I'll help however I can."

A Trail of Evidence

Tom points toward the shed. "Funny thing - I saw Mittens running toward the shed yesterday afternoon. She had something shiny in her mouth. I thought she'd caught a mouse or something."

You examine the ground carefully. The rain has softened the earth, revealing a trail of paw prints - leading directly to the shed door.

"That shed's been locked for months," Tom adds. "But Mittens... well, she's remarkably clever. There's a loose board at the back she uses to get in."

Clues Summary

  • Paw prints - Lead from the house to the shed
  • Tom's sighting - Mittens carrying something shiny
  • Loose board - Cat-sized entrance to the shed
  • Mittens' habit - Known for stealing and hiding things
