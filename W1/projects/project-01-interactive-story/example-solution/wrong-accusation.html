The Wrong Suspect

A Detective's Mistake

Jumping to Conclusions

"I know who took the brooch!" you announce confidently. "It was you!"

The accused looks at you with shock and hurt in their eyes. "What? I would never steal from Mrs. Blackwood! I've been loyal to this family for years!"

But you're certain. You call the police and have your suspect taken away for questioning. Mrs. Blackwood looks troubled but trusts your judgment as a professional detective.

A Costly Error

Hours later, you receive an embarrassing phone call. The police have released your suspect - there was no evidence against them. In fact, they had a solid alibi.

Mrs. Blackwood is understanding but disappointed. "I thought you were going to thoroughly investigate before making accusations," she says quietly. "Now an innocent person has been humiliated, and my brooch is still missing."

What Went Wrong

You made a classic detective mistake: accusing before investigating fully.

  • You didn't follow all the clues
  • You didn't check all the locations
  • You jumped to conclusions based on assumptions
  • The real thief is still out there with the brooch

Mrs. Blackwood politely asks you to leave. "I'll find another detective," she says. "One who will take the time to do this properly."

The Real Thief

As you walk to your car, you notice Mittens the cat sitting by the garden shed, watching you with what looks like amusement. She yawns, stretches, and slips through a gap in the shed wall.

Wait a minute...

But it's too late. You've lost the case, your reputation is damaged, and the truth remains hidden in that shed.
